About Changbai Mountain

Changbai Mountain is a perfect living showcase of the grandeur of natural landscapes. Chang Bai Shan (Eternally White Mountain) is named for its abundant white pumice and snow on the main peak. Renowned for rich natural resources and strong cultural element, It is not surprising that this nearly perfect reserve boasts 2596 kinds of wild plants and 2,442 kinds of wild animals, of which there are over 50 animals under first or second class protection at the national level. It enjoys the fame as one of top ten famous mountains of China. Besides, the mountain is the hometown of “king of beasts” manchurian tiger, the source land of “three treasures in Northeast China”, and the headstream of Songhua River, Tumen River, and Yalu River. It is also long considered to be a source of the Manchurian and Korean cultures. Changbai Mountain experiences big popularity among people from all over the world throughout the centuries. A trip here can make life long memories.

Scenic Spots

Tianchi Lake


With clouds everywhere, Tianchi Lake is like a jade in the sky. It is slightly elliptical and was formed in the crater of the mountain. Surrounded by 16 mountain peaks, Tianchi Lake is China’s largest crater lake with the tallest altitude and deepest water. It may rain very hard one minute, but you may soon find the sun shining. Sometimes you can even watch it rain in the west and shine in the east. Legend has it that there are monsters in this lake, which gives Tianchi Lake an air of mystery.
 

Alpine Garden


Alpine garden is amazing. Wildflower-lovers are never disappointed in July when the hillsides and peaks are splashed with blooming flowers. They come out, flower, set seeds, and go into dormancy quickly in the short frost-free season. Due to its unique altitude, climate and soil, there are no trees but a tapestry of continuous alpines over 10 hectares. Covered by over 170 kinds of tundra plants, it is a park in paradise. 
 

Changbai Waterfall


To the north of Tianchi Lake there exists an exit for the Chengcha River. Running gently for about 1,200 meters (0.74 miles), the river suddenly becomes a churning rapids and drops from a 68 meters (223 ft) high cliff. This is the famous Changbai Waterfall. Watching from far away, it appears like a white ribbon. As you approach it, you can hear the loud roar and feel the chilliness of the waterfall. Heifengkou on the main peak offers you the best perspective to view its splendor.
 

Changbai Mountain Grand Canyon


Stretching for over 70 kilometers, the widest spot of the Changbai Mountain Grand Canyon is around 300 meters, yet the narrowest is only a few meters. After volcanic eruptions, the ash and soil on lava stone surface are washed out by rivers and rains so as to form the canyon. It can be called a natural volcanic lava bonsai, integrating strange peaks, rare rocks, quiet valleys, beautiful waters and ancient trees.
